First Landing

Before Miku was released


Vocaloid was nothing more than a little-known program. Nico Nico Douga played a pivotal role in the recognition and popularity of the software. Shortly after its release, Nico Nico Douga users began uploading songs created with the software. According to Crypton, a popular video with a cartoon version of Miku holding a leek and singing Ievan Polkka presented the many possibilities of applying the software in the creation of multimedia content. As recognition and popularity grew, Nico Nico Douga became a place for collaborative content creation. Popular original songs written by one user could quickly generate artwork, 2D and 3D animation, and remixes by other users.

Initial Sales

Hatsune Miku's initial sales were too high


In the first 12 days of sales, nearly 3,000 sales reservations were made. This was approximately 1 sale in 250 within the total music software industry, being mentioned as an "impossible figure" by Watu Sasaki himself - the person in charge of planning and producing the company. On September 12, 2007, Amazon.co.jp said that sales of Hatsune Miku had reached 57,500,001 yen, making it the best-selling software at that time. On October 18, 2007, the Bulletin Board System website reported the suspicion that she was a victim of censorship by Google and Yahoo!, because Miku's images were not displayed by the image search system..
